Principal Investigator

Dr. Natasha Sekhon [say-kho] is an Assistant Professor of Geology at Occidental College. Natasha received her B.Sc. from the University of California, Irvine, followed by a M.Sc. and Ph.D. from the Institute for Geophysics and Department of Geological Sciences at the University of Texas, Austin, respectively. She was a postdoctoral researcher at Brown Univerisity as a Voss Postdoc Research Associate and Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow. Dr. Sekhon studies changes in the hydroclimate cycle through time using stable isotope and trace element geochemistry. In her free time, Natasha enjoys attending live performances, dancing, climbing or immersing herself in new cultures and languages! Download CV to make hyperlinks work.
Dr. Sekhon is on her pre-tenure Early Career Sabbatical for AY26/27 and will not be accepting any new students.

Quetzalli Feria-Galicia ('27)
Quetzalli is using rain water samples from Punjab, NW India to analyze pre-monsoon and monsoon precipitation during the Indian Summer Monsoon for stable isotopes. Her research will contribute to the ongoing scientific research on tropical cloud microphysics with the ultimate goal of understanding the past and future of the Indian Summer Monsoon.
As an Indigenous Chicana, Quetzalli’s work is driven by her connection to and respect for the Earth. She hopes to become a liaison between Indigenous communities and geologists, using two different forms of knowledge to mitigate climate change. In her free time Quetzalli enjoys crocheting, singing karaoke with friends, and taking her sassy dog to the beach.

Ginny Tomlinson ('28)
Ginny is a rising Junior majoring in Group Languages with a focus on Linguistics and Japanese and a Geology minor. She is interested in the relationship between the environment and human culture. In Summer 2026, Ginny assisted Dr. Sekhon in the Philippines for two weeks through the Faculty-Lead Richter Research program. The field expedition allowed the WICK Lab to check on instruments and collect new surface and river water samples to better understand the watershed dynamics between the surface and subsurface and groundwater and seawater. As an SRP Summer 2026 Fellow, Ginny is working on creating a local climate record from a stalagmite sample collected in the northeast Philippines.
In her free time, Ginny enjoys rugby, ultimate frisbee, making art and eating great food with friends. You can connect with Ginny on her Linkedin.

James Buellesbach ('28)
James is a rising Junior majoring in Biology with a minor in Geology. He is working Dr. Shana Goffredi from the Symbioxys lab and Dr. Sekhon to examine the diversity of microbial communities through carbonate karst cave systems. In Summer 2026, James travelled with Dr. Sekhon to the Philippines for two weeks through the Faculty-Lead Richter Research program. James collected water samples from the surface and subsurface to investigate relationships between microbes and trace elements in cave systems. As an SRP Summer 2026 Fellow, James will continue his geomicrobiology research to uncover how life persists in the subterreanean world.
When not working with bacteria and rocks, James can often be found on the track as a member of the Occidental college Track and Field team. Besides running, James spends his free time reading, listening to music and wearing mismatched socks.
